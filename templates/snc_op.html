Signals and Connectivity (S&C) Ops

This operation focus centers on advancing the frontiers of signal intelligence in next-generation networking infrastructure. It specializes in researching and developing cutting-edge methodologies for signal acquisition, fusion, processing, transmission, and reception within Smart Network Systems (SNS).

Research focus encompasses cognitive networks, smart grids, smart cities, and Industry 4.0 systems. A primary area of investigation is the enhancement of broadband wireless access through ultra-high frequency bands, such as Terahertz (THz) frequencies, leveraging advanced machine learning principles.

We are pioneering the development of hybridized network architectures that seamlessly integrate centralized and decentralized operational modalities. These systems build upon established distributed technologies, including blockchain and peer-to-peer (P2P) protocols, to create robust networking solutions.

The convergence of smart and hybridized networks presents unprecedented opportunities for creating resilient, self-adapting machine networks. These systems, which we designate as Architecturally Fluid Initiative Networks (ArcFIN), harness the resilience of decentralized systems while maintaining the efficiency of centralized architectures. Unlike conventional automated enterprise networks, ArcFIN systems uniquely enable large-scale adaptable network infrastructure utilizing edge computing principles.
